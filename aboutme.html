ABOUT ME

Hey there! It's nice to meet you!

My name is Zachary Rosen. I'm a Data Scientist and computational linguist with expertise in Language Modeling (from LLMs to earlier word vector models), NLU methods for group/consumer insights, and user behavior modeling. I tend to rely on a gammut of tools--no problem requires quite the same modeling approach--but what thematically ties my work together is my emphasis on making sense of human behavior using big data.

Currently, I'm embedded in Rick Dale's CoMind Lab at UCLA. My area of research is in quantiatively modeling how individuals come to a consensus on how they think about topics and issues as a community. If you're interested in practical applications of that, check out my resume! From the perspective of that work, there are a number of interesting ways of making sense of people's behavior online that have useful business applications.

If you're looking to learn a bit more about me, feel free to keep scrolling on this page, or check out my Curriculum Vitae in the link in the banner. You can get a quick summary of some of my more recent publications research initiatives via the "Current Initiatives" tab (link in the banner). And if you want to read about some nerdy, pop-sci data science experiments, head on over to my blog.

Let me know if you have any questions or want to collaborate on a particular project or idea. You can reach me by email at zachary.p.rosen[at]gmail.com.
