CURRENT INITIATIVES

Information Theoretic Approaches to Intragroup Communication
LLMs, Information Theory,

Human communication is incredibly dynamic. Work in areas like Communication Accommodation Theory (CAT), Conceptual Pact Theory (CPT), and Interactive Alignment Theory (IAT) posit that in order for conversation to unfold normally individuals have to align--to converge--how they discuss and understand their environment, mediated through communication.

An important--a primary--initiative in my work is in merging information theoretic approaches and contemporary language modeling technology (i.e. Transformer models and the occasional LLM) to better capture these moments of "convergence". From there, myself and collaborators work to capture what social factors in conversation might affect those measurements. This work allows myself and other researchers to better understand the relationship of ongoing discourse/conversation and speakers' social environment in a direct way that can have impacts of a variety of policies and decisions.

The effects of Hate Speech on Measures of Social Language Use
LLMs, Information Theory

Research into hate speech tends to focus on one of two things: (1) How hate speech affects the groups and identities targeted by it, and (2) what motivates individuals to use hate speech. What's missing from these conversations though is an understanding of how hate speech fundamentally shifts the discourse around it. Myself and collaborators at UCLA are working to unpack the ways hate speech changes the flow and exchange of information in web-based discourse. (see the next initiative statement).

Differences in LLM and Human Discourse Dynamics
LLMs, Information Theory, Pedagogy

Despite being lauded as open-domain conversational agents, LLMs exhibit strong differences from humans when it comes to engaging in prolonged discourse. Some of my recent work shows that these differences may be due to LLMs being incapable of replicating human motivations for introducing novelty into ongoing conversations.

In addition to these findings, I'm working on formal proofs showing that LLM behavior on typical NLP related tasks like intent classification and topic modeling, focusing on the information theoretic requirements of such tasks.

Socio-Semantic Networks with Large Language Models
LLMs, Graph-ML Methods, Monte Carlo Sampling

Using convergence analysis to induce edge weights between individuals based on text data. Requires the use of embeddings generated by large language models, information theoretic models of communication, and monte carlo sampling for testing and evaluation. I am working with collaborators right now on using this for bibliometric analyses of researcher outputs.
