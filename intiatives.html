CURRENT INITIATIVES

Social Influences Leading to the Generation of Hate Speech
LLMs, Information Theory

There is a wealth of work that seeks to create classifiers for the detection of hate speech. However, formally hate speech is notoriously hard to detect. Individuals who proliferate it are under constant pressure to change the means they use to express it. Myself and collaborators at UCLA are taking a different approach, and are using measurements of linguistic convergence and available meta data to better understand what social factors within groups facilitate the spread of hate-based content (see the next initiative statement).

Our results can have immediate impacts on content moderation in this domain by pointing policy makers towards strategies that stem the proliferation of hate speech more effectively.

Information Theoretic Approaches to Intragroup Communication
LLMs, Information Theory,

Human communication is incredibly dnymic. Work in areas like Communication Accommodation Theory (CAT), Conceptual Pact Theory (CPT), and Interactive Alignment Theory (IAT) posit that in order for conversation to unfold normally individuals have to align--to convergece--how they discuss and understand their environment, mediated through communication.

An important--a primary--initiative in my work is in merging information theoretic approaches and contemporary language modeling technology (i.e. Transformer models and the occasional LLM) to better capture these moments of "convergence". From there, it is possible to predict what social factors in conversation might affect those measurements. This work allows myself and other researchers to better understand the relationship of ongoing discourse/conversation and speakers' social environment

Socio-Semantic Networks with Large Language Models
LLMs, Graph-ML Methods, Monte Carlo Sampling

Using convergence analysis to induce edge weights between individuals based on text data. Requires the use of language models (either BERT or BLOOM), information theory, and monte carlo sampling for testing and evaluation. I am working with collaborators right now on using this for bibliometric analyses of researcher outputs.

Differences in LLM and Human Discourse Dynamics
LLMs, Information Theory, Pedagogy

Despite being lauded as open-domain conversational agents, LLMs exhibit strong differences from humans when it comes to engaging in prolonged discourse. Some of my recent work shows that these differences may be due to LLMs being incapable of replicating human motivations for introducing novelty into ongoing conversations.

In addition to these findings, I'm working on formal proofs showing that LLM behavior on typical NLP related tasks like intent classification and topic modeling, focusing on the information theoretic requirements of such tasks.
